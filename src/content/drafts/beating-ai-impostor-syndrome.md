## Introduction 

One of the psychologycal concepts with huge impact in my professional career has been the so called "Impostor Syndrome". In summary, is a mental process that provides you the wrong sense of being underqualified for most of your tasks and that eventually someone will notice it. 

This syndrome has been widely studied and tested and has a strong bond with multiple mental biases; especially the ones related with our skills self-perception. We consider what we know as too easy compared with what we don't and this gap provides us the false sense of less capacity than others. 

In the past months, with the arrival of AI, I have been observing an interesting version of this syndrome exacerbated: what I decided to call the AI Impostor Syndrome. 

### What is AI Impostor Syndrome?

While the standard or common Impostor Syndrome has its multiple root causes directly related with other human beings: early conditioning, high-pressure environments at work, social comparison or visibility, etc. the AI Impostor Syndrome is a battle started by a machine and lost by a person. 

There was a moment, in the AI tools race, that we cross a line: we started the process chatting with an AI, then we added that AI as a *copilot* of our daily tasks and finally we allowed it to become capable to do things *instead of us*.
